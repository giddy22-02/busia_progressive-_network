About Us

Busia Progressive Network (BPN) is a non-profit community-based organization that was registered in 2020 to respond to environmental conservation, education, poverty and psychosocial and mental health challenges in Busia and surrounding counties.

Busia and surrounding counties are plagued by adverse poverty due to a growing population against diminished resources. When the needs of different community groups are unmet, they succumb to stress and other mental health challenges.

To cope, they have exploited the available scarce resources in order to sustain themselves and give the children and adolescents an education and a better life. Unfortunately, this has left the environment grossly degraded and in dire need of restoration and conservation.

BPN leverages the networks of the Busia community, like-minded individuals and institutionsâ€™ goodwill, ability and potential to coalesce around issues affecting, especially children, and youths and the larger community to offer innovative and sustainable solutions to prevalent needs.

To respond to these multidimensional challenges, BPN works with experienced and proven institutions, local partners and government to undertake programmes that guarantee satisfactory service delivery and quality impact to the affected groups in the community.

Our programs and efforts are yielding great impacts among targeted community groups through resilience building when tackling daily challenges, poverty reduction, improved mental and psychosocial wellbeing and renewed efforts to ensure environmental restoration and conservation.

Continued collaborative efforts through capacity building, material support and information sharing are not only giving hope to many but laying a foundation for a Busia that is Healthy, Enlightened, socio-economically prosperous and environmentally viable.

Our Goal

To develop and implement interventions that guarantee human rights, enhance education and prosperity for all; and promote psychosocial wellbeing while reversing the negative effects of poverty and environmental degradation.

Vision

A healthy, enlightened, socio-economically prosperous and environmentally viable community.

Mission

To equip communities in Busia County and Kenya with the requisite knowledge, skills and support for socio-economic transformation, healthy living and environmental restoration and conservation.

Core Values

Accountability, Transparency: to ensure integrity is upheld and credibility is achieved, and Openness to criticism and embracing new ideas to promote progressive growth
Leadership: that listens, inspires and gives direction to the community to address its issues.
Professionalism and Innovation: In the quality of our work for effective service delivery
Community Involvement: for diverse strengths, voices, needs and backgrounds to ensure programme ownership and sustainability.
